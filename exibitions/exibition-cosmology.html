Cosmology

Cosmology is the study of the universe, including its origins, structure, and the mysteries of space, helping us understand how everything in the cosmos is connected.

The sky above us

Explore the wonders of our cosmos. Our fantastic exhibition, ‘The Sky Above Us’, explores the night sky and what we can see and know about the universe around us. We’ll locate the various constellations and galaxies that can be seen and learn a bit about the early navigators who used the stars to travel by.

Follow the journey of our solar exploration: from early Arab traders, to Galileo’s telescope, to the latest exploration of the planets in our solar system.

Did you know astronauts’ height can change while they're in space? In the microgravity environment, the spine stretches out, causing astronauts to grow up to 2 inches taller!

Do you think you can find the Big Dipper up in the sky? We have a huge colletion of telescopes you can test out!

Stars come in different colors depending on their temperature. Hotter stars appear blue or white, while cooler stars appear red. So, if you ever see a blue star twinkling in the sky, you’re looking at one that’s blazing hot!

The Big Bang Theory

One of the most important concepts in cosmology is the Big Bang Theory, which describes the birth of the universe about 13.8 billion years ago. According to this theory, the universe began as an incredibly hot and dense point, a singularity, before rapidly expanding in a process known as inflation.

As the universe expanded, it cooled, allowing for the formation of subatomic particles, atoms, and eventually stars and galaxies.

Blast off into the future! Explore the wonders of space and become the captain of your own spaceship in our out-of-this-world simulator!

The universe is still expanding! After the Big Bang, which happened around 13.8 billion years ago, the universe has been stretching out ever since. In fact, galaxies are moving away from us, and the farther they are, the faster they're moving!

Did you know rocket engines work by expelling gas to push the spaceship forward? In space, there's no air for traditional engines, so rockets rely on Newton's law: every action has an equal and opposite reaction!
