Biology & Medicine

From micro-organisms to the human body, major breakthroughs in biology are offering us unique insights into the great wonders of the tiny world.

The Evolution of Medicine

For many people their life expectancy is much longer and their quality of life much improved, thanks to the growth of our understanding of medicine. Over a series of exhibits we explore the history of medicine and take a look at some major breakthroughs including the discovery of penicillin and the first heart transplant.

Some of our earliest ancestors, like Australopithecus, walked on two legs, but still had long arms for climbing trees!

Did you know your heart beats about 100,000 times a day? That’s over 35 million times a year! It pumps about 2,000 gallons (7,570 liters) of blood throughout your body every single day, keeping you energized and healthy.

Did you know that the first medicine ever used was honey? Ancient Egyptians and other civilizations used honey to help heal wounds and soothe sore throats because it has natural healing powers!

Discover science in our hands-on lab

Many of our great medicinal discoveries have come not only from the lab, but also from observing animals in the wild. Often our fellow creatures have beat us to it.

Get your lab coat on and get ready for some awesome experiments! Our Science Lab is packed with activities that let you explore chemistry, physics, biology, and more. Whether you're mixing colorful liquids, exploring the mysteries of space, or testing out cool machines, there's always something new and exciting to try.

Ever seen how a grasshopper looks under a microscope? Experiment in our well-equipped laboratory.

Petri dishes were invented in 1887 by Julius Richard Petri to grow bacteria, and today they’re essential tools in science for growing microorganisms and even plants!

Delving into the fascinating world of liquids, experimenting with different combinations to uncover the science behind each reaction!

Did you know that your body has around 37.2 trillion cells? Each one plays a unique role in keeping you alive and functioning!
