Support us!

There are various ways you can support the museum. Donations are very welcome and are an important way we keep this museum open and accessible to the whole community.

You can also support us by donating items of interest to the museum’s collections. If you have some item or collection that you think others would enjoy, please let us know by contacting our Collections Department and they will be able to assist you.

How your donation will help

50,-

Helps provide educational resources to schoolchildren and underserved communities.

100,-

You’ll help fund local STEM research projects that directly impact our community.

200,-

Provides funding for scholarships and internships that support emerging scientists from diverse backgrounds

500,-

It could fund materials and lab supplies for early-stage research, which could eventually lead to larger grants or breakthroughs.

1000,-

It could help purchase antibodies to track the immune response. and reagents needed to prepare cell cultures.

Volunteer

A number of people volunteer their time and effort to keep the displays in good order and ready for visitors to come and enjoy. Volunteering has its perks including getting to see behind the scenes of a working museum, access to staff-only lectures, and a monthly lunch where all staff and volunteers come together to discuss ideas for future exhibits and strategies for the museum. You can help volunteer in a number of different spheres. Please contact us if you’d like to find out more about how you can get involved.

Become a Junior Scientist: Internship Opportunities!

Are you a curious explorer who loves science? Do you want to get a behind-the-scenes look at how a science center works? Our Junior Scientist Internship Program is the perfect opportunity for kids who want to learn, create, and grow in a real science environment!

What’s It All About?

As an intern, you’ll have the chance to dive into exciting science projects, help create fun exhibits, and work alongside our team of scientists, educators, and designers. You’ll explore topics like physics, biology, chemistry, and even space exploration! Whether you're helping with hands-on experiments or designing your own mini project, you’ll be making a difference at the science center.

Why Apply?

  • Learn by Doing: You won’t just read about science—you’ll get to experience it firsthand!
  • Get Creative: Help design exhibits and activities that other kids will love.
  • Work with Experts: Our science team is passionate about teaching and inspiring the next generation of scientists.
  • Build Skills for the Future: Gain teamwork, leadership, and problem-solving skills that will help you in school and beyond.

What Will You Do as an Intern?

  • Assist with interactive science demos and experiments.
  • Help set up and maintain new exhibits and displays.
  • Share your love for science by leading activities or giving tours to visitors.
  • Participate in special science workshops and events.

Who Can Apply?

Our internship program is open to kids ages 10-16 who are passionate about science and want to get hands-on experience in a fun, dynamic environment. No previous experience is required—just bring your curiosity and enthusiasm!

How to Apply?

Ready to jump into the world of science? To apply, just fill out the internship application form on our website. Be sure to tell us why you’re excited about science and what you hope to learn from this experience. We can’t wait to hear from you!
